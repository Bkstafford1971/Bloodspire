Players Connection

Races

Choosing a race is one of the most important decisions you make when creating a warrior. BLOODSPIRE features 10 playable races, each with unique strengths, weaknesses, and preferred ways of fighting. No race is strictly better than the others — they are balanced around different playstyles. A race that excels in one area usually has meaningful trade-offs elsewhere.

Racial modifiers influence combat performance, training speed, injury resistance, and how well a warrior uses certain weapons or armor. These effects are applied automatically in fights and training — you don’t add or subtract numbers manually from the stats you see during creation.

Human

The adaptable everyman.

Humans are the most flexible race in the arena. They lack extreme strengths or glaring weaknesses, making them excellent for new managers or players who want reliable, well-rounded warriors.

Key Traits

  • Train attributes faster than most other races
  • Suffer fewer permanent injuries over time
  • Perform solidly with almost any weapon, armor, or fighting style

In Practice: A Human warrior improves more reliably through training and tends to stay competitive longer because injuries accumulate more slowly.

Half-Orc

Pure brute force personified.

Half-Orcs are the heaviest hitters in the Bloodspire. They deliver devastating damage and can absorb tremendous punishment.

Key Traits

  • Exceptional damage output and durability
  • Slower attack speed and reduced ability to dodge or parry

In Practice: Half-Orcs excel when wielding heavy two-handed weapons (Great Axe, Maul, War Hammer).

Halfling

Infuriatingly hard to hit, but extremely fragile.

Halflings are small, quick, and slippery — masters of hit-and-run tactics.

Key Traits

  • Outstanding dodge and attack speed
  • Very low durability and damage output

Dwarf

The ultimate tank.

Key Traits

  • Highest durability and strong parrying ability
  • Can wear heavier armor than Strength allows
  • Reduced dodging capability

Half-Elf

Versatile and capable all-rounder.

Key Traits

  • Small useful bonuses to rate, dodge, and damage
  • Can use slightly heavier weapons than Strength suggests

Elf

Speed and grace.

Key Traits

  • Excellent speed, dodge, and attack rate
  • Strong affinity for dual-wielding and light blades
  • Very fragile

Goblin

Tiny, dirty, and fast.

Key Traits

  • High initiative and attack speed
  • Mastery with thrown weapons and scavenging gear

Gnome

Small but surprisingly tough tacticians.

Key Traits

  • Good durability for their size
  • Strong counter-attacking and parrying ability

Lizardfolk

Savage reptilian predators.

Key Traits

  • High durability and natural scale armor
  • Powerful natural weapons (claws, tail)
  • Lighter armor layers effectively over scales

Tabaxi

Lightning-quick acrobatic felines.

Key Traits

  • Exceptional dodge and initiative
  • Acrobatic prowess (hard to knock down)
  • One powerful Frenzy burst per fight

Weapons, Armor & Helms

Strength Carry Capacity

Every warrior has a limit to how much weight they can carry effectively, based on their Strength. Heavy gear requires high Strength, otherwise speed and power are reduced.

Dwarves: Wear one armor tier heavier than Strength normally allows.

Lizardfolk: Lighter armor layers on top of natural scales with less speed penalty.

Armor Types (Lightest to Heaviest)

  • Cloth — Minimal protection, no speed penalty.
  • Leather — Light and flexible modest protection.
  • Cuir Boulli — Hardened leather, balanced mid-tier.
  • Brigandine — Riveted metal plates over leather.
  • Scale — Solid protection, starts to slow warrior.
  • Chain — Classic mail, good all-around protection.
  • Half-Plate — Excellent protection, significant speed cost.
  • Full Plate — Ultimate protection, extremely heavy.

Helm Types

  • Leather Cap — No speed penalty.
  • Steel Cap — Decent protection, almost no penalty.
  • Helm — Standard metal helmet.
  • Camail — Chain protection for neck and shoulders.
  • Full Helm — Maximum protection, highest speed penalty.
