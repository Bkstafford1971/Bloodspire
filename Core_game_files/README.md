# Bloodspire User Guide

## What is Bloodspire?

Bloodspire is a **tactical gladiatorial combat simulator** set in a brutal fantasy arena. You take on the role of a **manager** who recruits, equips, trains, and commands a team of five warriors. Your goal is to guide your team to glory by winning fights, climbing the rankings, and building a legendary reputation in the Bloodspire Arena.

Each turn, your warriors are matched against opponents from other managers (or the arena’s own forces). You control:

- **Gear** (weapons, armor, and helms)
- **Fighting styles and strategies**
- **Training focus** (which skills or attributes to improve)

The arena is unforgiving. Warriors can be permanently injured or slain. Smart management, clever strategy choices, and understanding your warriors’ strengths are the keys to long-term success.

## Playable Races

There are **8 playable races** in Bloodspire. Each has distinct strengths, weaknesses, and playstyles. No race is strictly “best” — success depends on how you build and support them.

| Race          | Playstyle Summary                          | Key Strengths                              | Key Weaknesses                              | Best For                                      |
|---------------|--------------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------|
| **Human**     | Versatile all-rounders                     | Balanced stats, trains attributes faster, fewer permanent injuries | No extreme advantages                       | New players, flexible teams                   |
| **Half-Orc**  | Brutal powerhouses                         | Very high damage, high durability          | Slow, poor dodge and parry                  | Aggressive, high-damage builds                |
| **Halfling**  | Evasive tricksters                         | Extremely hard to hit, very fast           | Extremely fragile, low damage               | Hit-and-run, defensive counter teams          |
| **Dwarf**     | Ironclad tanks                             | Highest HP, master parriers, strong armor use | Very slow, poor dodge                       | Defensive, grind-out fights                   |
| **Half-Elf**  | Skilled and adaptable                      | Good weapon handling, solid all-around     | No standout extremes                        | Balanced or technical teams                   |
| **Elf**       | Elegant speed fighters                     | Fast attack rate, excellent dual-wielding  | Very fragile                                | High-mobility, slashing teams                 |
| **Goblin**    | Dirty, tricky fighters                     | Extremely fast, strong with thrown weapons | Very weak and fragile                       | Opportunistic, ranged harassment              |
| **Gnome**     | Clever tacticians                          | Strong counterstrikes, good vs aggression  | Small and somewhat fragile                  | Defensive counter-punch teams                 |
| **Lizardfolk**| Savage reptilian predators                 | Tough, natural armor & weapons, relentless | Slower to accelerate                        | Durable, close-range brawlers                 |
| **Tabaxi**    | Lightning-quick acrobats                   | Best evasion, high initiative, burst speed | Fragile, tires in long fights               | Evasive, high-mobility strike teams           |

### Quick Race Tips:
- **Humans** — Most beginner-friendly and very adaptable.
- **Dwarves & Lizardfolk** — Excellent tanks but need good positioning.
- **Halflings, Elves & Tabaxi** — Rely on speed and evasion; very fragile if hit.
- **Half-Orcs & Goblins** — High-risk, high-reward fighters.
